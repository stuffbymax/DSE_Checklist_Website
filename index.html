DSE Workstation Checklist

Ensure your workstation complies with the Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations.

1. Display Screen

Use a mirror to check for reflections. Move the screen or the desk if necessary.

The top of the screen should be at or just below eye level.

A clean screen is essential for reducing eye strain.

3. Keyboards

This is a requirement unless using a portable device where this is impracticable.

Tilt need not be built-in but should be available if needed.

Ensure there is enough space for hands and wrists by moving the screen back if necessary. A wrist rest may be needed for thick, raised keyboards.

Training can prevent poor technique, such as hands bent at the wrist, hitting the keys too hard, or overstretching the fingers.

Ensure the keyboard is clean. If characters aren't readable, consider modifying or replacing the keyboard. A matt finish can reduce glare.

4. Mouse, Trackball, etc.

If the user is having issues, consider trying a different device. General-purpose devices like the mouse and trackball come in many shapes and sizes. For certain tasks, alternatives like touch screens may be more suitable.

Position the device as close as possible to the keyboard to avoid overreaching. Training may help prevent arm strain, encourage relaxed arm and wrist posture, and remind users not to leave their hand on the device when not in use.

Support can come from the desk surface or chair arm. If not, a separate wrist or forearm support may be needed.

Check if the device needs cleaning (e.g., mouse ball or rollers), and ensure the work surface is suitable. A mouse mat may help.

Users may need training to adjust settings like pointer speed and accuracy.

4. Software

Software should help minimize stress and be user-friendly.

Ensure users are familiar with the software's features.

6. Furniture and Chair

If the workspace is crowded, consider moving printers and reference materials elsewhere. Rearranging power and telecom sockets may help relocate equipment. Ensure there is flexibility for rearrangement.

Rearrange equipment and frequently used items to be within easy reach. A document holder might be useful to minimize uncomfortable head and eye movements.

Consider using mats or blotters to reduce glare and reflections on surfaces.

The chair should be stable and allow for adjustments. If the user is uncomfortable, the chair may need repairing or replacing.

Ensure that the adjustment mechanisms of the chair are functional. If not, repairs or replacements may be necessary.

6. Environment

Ensure there is space to move, stretch, and fidget. Reorganize the office layout if necessary, and check for obstructions. Tidy up cables to avoid trip or snag hazards.

Users should be able to control light levels by adjusting blinds or light switches. If necessary, consider shading or repositioning light sources or providing desk lamps, but ensure they donâ€™t cause glare.

DSE and other equipment may dry the air. Circulate fresh air, and consider adding plants or a humidifier if air discomfort is severe.

Consider more ventilation or air conditioning, especially if there is a lot of electronic equipment in the room. Alternatively, relocate users away from heat sources.

If noise is an issue, try moving sources of noise (like printers) away from users. If not possible, consider soundproofing the environment.
